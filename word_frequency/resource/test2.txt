Quality Control & Quality Assurance
Quality Assurance:
Quality Control:
A set of activities designed to evaluate a developed work product.
Differences:
A set of activities designed to ensure that the development and/or
maintenance process is adequate to ensure a system will meet its
objectives.
QA focus on the process elements of a project. QC activities focus on
finding defects in specific deliverables.
QA makes sure you are doing the right things, the right way, QC makes
sure the results of what you've done are what you expected.
QA is process oriented and QC is product oriented.
